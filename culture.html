Culture and Traditions of Kazakhstan before Independence

Kazakhstan has a rich cultural and historical heritage that predates its independence in 1991. country's culture has been shaped by a combination of nomadic, Turkic, Persian, Mongol, nd Russian influences. Here are some key aspects of the culture and traditions of Kazakhstan before its independence.

Traditional Clothing:

For Men

Shapan: The shapan is a long, robe-like garment that reaches the ankles. It is typically worn over other layers of clothing and is fastened at the waist with a wide belt.

Koylek: This is a long-sleeved, high-necked shirt worn underneath the shapan. It is often made of silk or cotton and serves as a base layer.

Kalpak: The kalpak is a traditional hat made of fur or felt. It has a distinctive square shape and is often worn by men as a symbol of identity.

For Women

Saukele: The saukele is a headdress worn by Kazakh women, particularly during weddings. It is made of various materials, including silver, gold, and adorned with gems. The saukele is a symbol of wealth and marital status.

Koylek: Similar to the men's shirt, the koylek for women is a long-sleeved garment worn under the outer layers. It is often decorated with embroidery and colorful patterns.

Shapan: Women's shapan is similar to that of men but is often more elaborately decorated, especially for special occasions.

Festivals and Celebrations

Nauryz Meyrami

Nauryz Meyrami is indeed one of the most important and widely celebrated festivals in Kazakhstan. It marks the coming of spring and the renewal of nature. The festival has roots in ancient traditions and is celebrated not only in Kazakhstan but also in several other Central Asian countries.

Traditional Food: Special dishes are prepared for Nauryz, and it is common for families to gather and share festive meals. Beshbarmak, a dish made with boiled meat (usually lamb or beef) and served with pasta, is a staple during the festival.

Traditional Games: Various traditional games and competitions take place during Nauryz. These games often have historical significance and are enjoyed by people of all ages. Popular activities include horse racing, wrestling, and other traditional sports.

Weddings

Kyz Uzatu: This is a tradition where the groom, accompanied by his close friends and relatives, goes to the bride's house to ask for her hand in marriage. The bride's female relatives playfully try to prevent the groom from entering, and negotiations take place before the bride is finally brought out.

Wedding Feast: The wedding feast is a significant part of the celebration, with traditional foods, music, and dance. Beshbarmak is often served during wedding celebrations as well.
